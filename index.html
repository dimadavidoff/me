The saying “man is like a child when it comes to his interests” suggests that human beings — no matter their age or level of maturity — often approach their passions with the same curiosity and enthusiasm as a child.

This comparison highlights a positive quality: the ability to retain a sense of wonder throughout life. At the same time, adults bring depth, experience, and reflection to their interests — approaching them not just with excitement, but with understanding and intention.

In essence, this idea embraces the duality of human nature — where childlike fascination and grown-up perspective coexist, enriching the way we explore the world and ourselves.


About Me

"As one of the great minds once said: it’s wonderful to invent yourself, but perhaps even more remarkable to truly understand and appreciate what others have created." Hi, my name is Dimitri Davidoff.

My life has always been filled with creativity and inspiration drawn from the works of others. As a naturally curious person, I’ve always tried to explore the essence, depth, and origins of everything I’m passionate about.

When I got into music, I didn’t just listen — I explored the stories behind the songs, the composers, original versions, and rare covers. This led me to create custom album compilations, complete with sound processing, promotion, and graphic design.

That creative process sparked my interest in digital tools. I began building websites on WordPress and learned Photoshop and other Adobe programs on my own.

I approach every interest with the same depth — whether reading the full works of Jack Vance after watching Game of Thrones, or diving into software testing and user experience. What connects all these paths is a drive for understanding, creation, and constant self-development.

Photography has always been a deeply fulfilling and creative pursuit for me. It’s more than just capturing moments — it’s a way to express emotion, tell stories, and share how I see the world.

I’ve been passionate about photography since childhood. I even remember the days of using film cameras and developing photos myself in a bathroom darkroom lit by a red lamp — there’s something deeply nostalgic and almost magical about that process.

Whether through analog or digital lenses, photography remains a meaningful way for me to connect with the world and preserve the moments that matter.

One of my core interests is conceptual art — a space where many genres and disciplines come together. For me, it doesn’t matter whether I’m designing a logo, building a website, assembling a music compilation, or crafting furniture by hand. It all begins with an idea.

That idea is first captured on paper — as a sketch, a note, a plan — and then brought to life through thoughtful design and execution. Teaching myself Adobe tools opened doors to freelance design work and creative freedom.

I’m also passionate about music and love curating compilations across genres and moods. Sometimes, a single track inspires an entire set. Each mix is edited like a story, with a theme and visual identity. In the CD era, I produced physical editions with custom covers and shared them with friends.

Hiking & Exploration

Hike 1

Exploring new places — whether cities, forests, or mountain trails — has always been a vital part of my life. I love the sense of discovery, even when it’s just a quiet path off the beaten track or a scenic overlook that others might miss.

Hiking gives me a way to reconnect with nature, reset my focus, and challenge my endurance. I often plan routes with elevation gain and long distances, enjoying both the physical activity and the mental clarity it brings.

These journeys also inspire my creativity. I often take my camera with me — capturing moments along the trail adds another layer of meaning to the experience.

Whether traveling abroad or exploring local landscapes, I’m driven by curiosity, a desire to observe, and a love of immersing myself in unfamiliar environments.

I’ve been practicing WingTsun since 2017, and over time it has become more than just a martial art — it’s become part of who I am.

We train as part of a European WingTsun organization, with regular seminars held in Switzerland and weekly classes in Israel. While you might not often spot me in group photos, that’s because I serve as the official photographer for WingTsun Israel — combining two of my passions.

WingTsun is known for its direct, efficient approach to self-defense and close-range combat. It emphasizes adaptability, precision, and inner focus — all qualities I value both on and off the mat.
